Redesigning a Bank Loyalty Site

Brief

Affinity Solutions works with Financial Institutions, National Retailers & Brands and Media companies. This project was a redesign for a Bank on our loyalty platform. The client had done heuristic analysis to discover critical issues and wanted to fix those issues along with others.

Role

UX, UI, Client Management, HTML Prototyper

Team

Graphics Designer, Jr UX Designer, Copyrighter, Account Manager, 2 Developers

Timeline

8 weeks for design
2 weeks for HTML

Goal: Redesign the site to make it more usable and intuitive: reducing user friction and increasing engagement.

Process

1

Heuristic Analysis

Client performed heuristic analysis and noticed various issues. Their designers made suggestions for the site

2

High Level Requirements

We collaborated with suggestions using UX best practices and our expertise to develop requirements

3

Initial Wires

We created wires based on the agreed upon requirements after consulting developers

4

Usability Study

We proposed a 10 user, 2 week usability study, iterated on the design, increasing perceived value and fixing the IA.

5

Visual Design

Client was very happy with the design and it improved the relationship with client, leading to other opportunities.

6

Functional Specifications

We wrote the specs using the results from the usability testing and best practices, continuing the theme of max perceived value.

7

Implementation

I provided an HTML prototype, worked with the developers on the finer details of the experience to ensure top quality including accessibility.

Affinity Solutions hosts loyalty platforms where users can earn more points or cash back by shopping through our “mall” to earn additional bonus points by using their card. The client had a hired UX expert to do a heuristic analysis of the Mall and provide suggestions, this was the basis for the redesign. The design had to be : accessible, usable, follow all branding requirements and meet legal compliance. We worked together with the requirements and suggested some of our own based on our intimate knowledge of the platform and created medium fidelity wires. We knew it was risky to do a major IA redesign without usability testing so we pushed the client to allow a 2-week study with anyone we could find to do the usability test.
