Client: globalFEST—Site Redesign

Brief

globalFEST’s website needs to reflect the prestige, industry credibility and the growing role it plays in supporting and exposing the world music community.

Goal: Get more users, infiltrate the market.

Team: Me, Keith, Andres

ANALYSIS OF CURRENT SITE

Content Strategy

  • Only 3 of 9 non-industry users could correctly identify what globalFEST was based on the site.
  • globalFEST was explained differently on different pages, in more than 5 different ways.

Usability Testing

CO-DESIGN

We met with the globalFEST team to figure out the Information Architecture and the journey of a globalFEST artist.

USER RESEARCH

We researched users in and outside of the globalFEST community, we wanted to make sure people's praise and appreciation of globalFEST did not bias them to minimize their pain points, especially when it came to the artists.

ITERATIONS – HOME PAGE

  1. Explain what globalFEST is
  2. Find out more about gF
  3. Explain what else gF does
  4. More of what gF does &
  5. Media from artists
  6. Footer navigation
  1. Strategy change, we show what gF is through content/images, not words so users can better understand what gF is.
  2. Most recent event, keeping the home page fresh.
  3. Featured artists of the week
  4. Credibility
  5. Link to artists added

ITERATIONS - EVENTS PAGE

  1. Unused navigation items moved to footer to keep users focused and reduce choices presented
  2. Elements re-ordered to represent the core of gF. Also “EVENTS” now goes to a landing page
  3. Left aligned for consistency
  4. Upcoming events keep the page fresh
  5. Explanation of different events

FINAL SITE MAP

FINAL RESULTS

Usability Tests Improvement

Final Recommendations

  • Create a visual brand. Then take that brand and apply to the website.
  • Create the content first. Try to create a consistent message about what gF is.
  • Keep using WordPress as it’s easy to update content.
