Project—Location Based Networking App

Brief

Applying for jobs can seem hopeless for some users, furthermore applying online without any connections can seem like you are sending your resume to a blackhole. Users report that they receive a lot of annoying or irrelevant messages on their professional networking platform. We sought to solve both these issues by designing a location based networking app called Espresso.

Project Goal: Help users save time by creating useful and meaningful professional connections.

Target User: Job seekers who want to build strong connections along the way.

Project Timeline

Team: Fernando, Kathleen, Corey

Problem Exploration Exercise

I led the team through the problem exploration exercise. This aligned the team on the problem we were interested in and gave us ideas of what to ask in the surveys and user interviews.

Problem Space

Current networking platforms have a decent amount of spam and mass communications, leading to people ignoring their inbox and then networking through other channels.

Goal Space

  • Connecting with people should be fun and enjoyable.
  • People should get value out of communicating with others who have a genuine interest in the field and others.
  • People shouldn’t have to deal with communications that are not relevant to them.
  • Conversations they have online should inspire them and help them to grow professionally.

Consequence

  • People will stop wasting time and start making more meaningful connections.
  • It will help people save time and energy to focus on things that matter.
  • It will help foster more organic connections increasing it’s user base and move it away from a mere place to host your profile and find others.
  • It would benefit the world by providing a channel for people to learn from each other and grow in a honest organic manner.

Gaps and Barriers

  • Social — People are on information overload, do they really want another application on their phone popping up, yet another way for people to get in touch with them.
  • Technological — We have great spam filtering, but can we tell the intention of someone when they send a message? Google is starting to do it with classifying things into promotions, forums and “important” maybe that is what LinkedIn needs.
  • Physical — Because of people’s limited time , no matter how many networking events you have, it is hard for people to come together.

User Research

We did surveys and user interviews to better understand user's experience while networking. We received 40 responses and conducted 14 user interviews.

Survey Summary

  • 60% of people use text or messaging apps as a primary means of communications with their family and friends.
  • 74% of people have been to at least 1 networking events in the past 3 months.
  • 77% of people use LinkedIn as their networking platform.
  • 48% of people reported getting spam or annoying messages on their networking platform.

Interview Insights

  • A lot of users had trouble networking at networking events.
  • Users like to be mentord and want a mentor to help guide their careers.
  • Networking online can be painful and seem hopeless.
  • Some people don’t have time to network as much as they would like to.

Wireframing & Usability

Onboarding

The user testing of the final prototype went well, except for the on-boarding process. We originally asked users to login , setup a profile enable notifications, enable location services. All before we showed them any value on using this application. Therefore, we delayed on-boarding until after they see the value in the application.

Conclusion

Users understood the app overall and were delighted by seeing the favorite drink of others. As the design process is never finished there are various opportunities left after the completion of this project.

Next Steps

  • Information architecture study on the roles and industries.
  • Potential gamification to incentivize those with little time to talk to others.
  • Comparative research of other location based dating applications to see what features could be used to improve the experience.
  • Research on the privacy concerns of users considering the following options show their location distance the last the time they were online.
  • Research on what an organic conversation is comprised of. By chance? Meeting someone that is like you in a different way? Something in common other than profession, school or workplace?
