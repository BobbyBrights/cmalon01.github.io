Back in middle school…

I got my first zenith computer and I learned how to program in QBASIC. I was fascinated by writing code.

Then in high school…
I learned Visual Basic, C++ and Java

I was supposed to do computers for the rest of my life. So I chose to do Computer Engineering at NYU Engineering.

I learned about the computer from the electron to the high level OS.

My passion was stronger than ever. Parallel systems, computer architecture, the cell processor.

So I started my PhD in Computer Science with a focus in Security.

I learned about the problems with security in hardware and software and the “evil users” always undoing what us security folk create.

I did an internship at Intel working on the same problem of software security. At one of the talks I saw Genevive Bell talk.

“the future of computing is experiences?”

I couldn't believe it at the time. I saw computers as a means to end as a way to get the desired ouput from the input.

BUT.. the seed was planted.

Finally I decided to leave the PhD program. It was hard to quit.

But I had to take care of the feeling that I was missing something...

What was the purpose of everything engineers do?

Why produce faster chips when the algorithms they run still aren’t fit for humans (“the user”)

…On the side I had been doing web development to make ends meet. Every computer geek knows how to make a website right?

<html>
Just do it…
</html>

EVEN I FELL VICTIM TO THIS FALSE BELIEF.

Yes we can technically build websites, but will that website matter?

I finally found the missing link was considering the User. The person on the other end of the screen.

So I asked my client.. why is this here? why are we doing that?

Soon enough I got an answer 'we don't know'. I set out to change that.

We had lots of discussions and finally began to see eye to eye.

You can take a look at the final product.

The more I read about UX..the more I felt this is what I was missing. But I couldn’t have gotten there by just happening upon UX. I had to discover for myself that it was important.

I'm almost done taking the User Experience Design immersive at General Assembly.

User Research, July 17th, 2015
